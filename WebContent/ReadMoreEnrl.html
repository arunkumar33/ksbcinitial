Admission and Enrolment of Advocates

A citizen of India who has completed the age of 21 years and has obtained the degree in law from any recognized University established in India or out side India which is recognized by the Bar Council of India is eligible for enrolment.

Disposal of applications

The State Bar Council would place the application received by the applicants before its Enrolment Committee constituted by the State Bar Council from time to time as per the provisions of the Advocates Act. The Enrolment Committee is empowered to conduct the refresher course for the fresh graduates on the date of enrolment in the morning session. Procedure :

The Enrolment Committee shall conduct an interview of the applicant in order to consider the application for his enrolment If the Enrolment Committee refuses to enroll any of the applicants, such applications would be referred to the Bar Council of India for its opinion. The Enrolment Committee of the State Bar Council disposes the applications in accordance with the rules framed by the State Bar Council as well as the Bar Council of India. Administration of oath

Administration of oath

The applicant shall take oath compulsorily administered by the Enrolment Committee. Certificate of Enrolment

Certificate of Enrolment

The Bar Council would issue a Certificate of Enrolment and Welfare Fund Certificate in the prescribed form by the State Bar Council to the applicant as soon as he is enrolled as an advocate by the Enrolment Committee. After the enrolment, the advocates shall collect the Enrolment Certificate as well as the Welfare Fund Certificate from the Enrolment Committee.

Transfer of Enrolment from our State Roll to another
Any Advocate whose name is entered as an advocate on the roll of Karnataka State Bar Council may make an application in the
prescribed form to the Bar Council of India for the transfer his name from that roll to roll of any
other State Bar council by paying the prescribed fee to the Bar Council of India as well as the Karnataka State Bar Council.
The applicant shall obtain the NOC from our Bar Council if he wants to get transfer from this Bar Council. For obtaining NOC the applicant shall pay a fee of Rs. 2000/- through on line. Transfer from any other State Bar council Roll to our State Council

RIGHT TO PRACTISE
Any Advocate enrolled in the Bar Council shall be entitled to right of practice through out India in all Courts including the Supreme Court of India, any Tribunals before any other authority or person before by whom such advocate is by or under any law for the time being in force entitled to practice.
