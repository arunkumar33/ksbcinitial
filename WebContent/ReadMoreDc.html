Discipline of Advocates

An advocate shall obey and follow the standards of professional conduct and etiquette rules framed by the Bar Council of India under Sec. 49 (1) ( c) of the Advocates Act, 1961 (See the Rules mentioned in Chapter II of the Bar Council of India Rules).
An Advocate shall, at all times, comfort himself in a manner befitting his status as an officer of the Court, a privileged member of the community, and a gentleman, bearing in mind that what may be lawful and moral for a person who is not a member of the Bar, or for a member of the Bar in his non professional capacity may still be improper for an Advocate. Without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing obligation, an advocate shall fearlessly uphold the interest of his client, and in his conduct conform to the rules hereinafter mentioned both in letter and in spirit.
The rules hereinafter mentioned contain canons of conduct and etiquette adopted as general guides, yet the specific mention thereof shall not be construed as a denial of the existence of others equally imperative though not specifically mentioned.

  1. The Seven Commandments viz.
  2. 1. Duty to the Court
  3. 2. Duty to the Client
  4. 3. Duty to opponent
  5. 4. Duty to Colleagues
  6. 5. Duty in imparting training
  7. 6. Duty to render legal aid
  8. 7. Restriction on their employments

(Details of which are found in the Advocates Act, 1961- One go through the details in the pdf file to this by clicking here) An advocate is liable for punishment for violating the etiquette rules framed by the Bar Council of India for professional misconduct and also for other misconduct under Sec. 35 of the Advocates Act, 1961. Any public or Officer of the Court can file a complaint in the prescribed form to the State Bar Council by paying the prescribed fee of Rs. 1000/- or any amount fixed by the Bar Council from time to time. Complaint can be filed through online or in person.

After registration of the complaint, the Bar Council will issue notice to the respondent advocate named in the complaint for submitting his explanation to the complaint. After the receipt of the explanation, the Bar Council in its General Council Meeting will consider the complaint and decide it whether an enquiry is required or not as contemplated under law. If the Bar Council accepts the complaint the matter will be referred to Disciplinary Committee constituted by it from time to time to dispose of the cases as enumerated under the Act.

The Bar Council shall refer the matter to one of the Committees for disposal of the matter in accordance with the Advocates Act and rules framed by the Bar Council of India from time to time for the disposal of the cases. The Disciplinary Committees will dispose of the matter within a period of one year from the date of reference of the complaint to the Disciplinary committee by the State Bar Council. The Disciplinary Committees will conduct its meeting on every Sunday or any other holiday as decided by them from 11 a.m. onwards by issuing notice to respective parties either through post or email or by sending sms or by calling through mobile.

The complainant and the respondent will be provided an opportunity to adduce evidence before the disciplinary committee to prove their case. The Disciplinary Committee after hearing the arguments would decide the complaint on merits. If the complaint is not decided within the period of One year, the Disciplinary Committee of the Bar Council will refer the matter to Bar Council of India for disposal.

Thereupon the Bar Council of India will dispose off the matter through their Disciplinary Committees constituted under the Act and Rules.

An appeal against the order of the Disciplinary Committee shall lie to the Bar Council of India within 90 days from the date of communication. Copies of the judgments and deposition and other documents will be supplied to the persons applying on the payment of the prescribed fee.
