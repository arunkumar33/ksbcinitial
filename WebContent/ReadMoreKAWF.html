Welfare Schemes to the Advocates on the Rolls of our Council

There are two Welfare Schemes formulated by the Karnataka State Bar Council.

Bar Council of India Advocates Welfare Fund Scheme :
      The Scheme was formulated by the Karnataka State Bar Council with the approval of the Bar Council of India in order to give financial assistance to the practicing advocates suffering from serious ailment.
The financial assistance provided only once in life time of an advocate. Under the scheme an advocate is entitled for a financial assistance not exceeding Rs. 50000/- when an advocate suffering from serious ailment as described here under.
"Serious Ailment" means undergoing amputation of limbs suffering from paralysis, affecting mobility or speech, bypass / open heart surgery / angioplasty, failure of the kidney suffering from cancer, HIV-AIDS, brain tumor, mental disorder, liver cirrhosis requiring hospitalization. "

But, the advocate who have enrolled on the Rolls of Bar Council after attaining an age of 40 years or who has voluntarily suspended practice for the purpose of employment, avocation or business or transfer from the other State Bar Councils, is only entitled for a maximum sum of Rs. 20000/- only.
The applicant shall apply for financial assistance in the prescribed form published in the web site and addressed to the Secretary duly certified by the Bar Association.
The application will be placed before the Committee constituted by the Bar Council for deciding the claim made by the Advocates.

Karnataka Advocates Welfare Fund
(Act 2 of 1985 as amended on 6.8.2010)
The Bar Council has formulated the Welfare Fund Scheme in order to provide social security to the legal profession by providing for payment of lumpsum amount after cessation of practice by its member and also for payment to the dependents of its Members, if they were to die during their practice.

The Advocates Welfare Fund is constituted out of among other emolument, fees and voluntary contribution and grant from Government. The Bar Council has also introduced the Welfare Fund Stamps so as to generate revenue to the Fund. The advocates across the State at present are affixing Rs. 10/- on vakalath in all Courts and Rs. 5/- on IAs. During the commencement of the scheme we were paying meager financial benefit to the families of the deceased Advocates and also who are retiring from the practice. The scheme has been amended and renewed from time to time looking into the growth of the corpus, income generated from the corpus and resource generated from the sale of stamps.

Under the present Act, 2010 (28/2010) our Bar Council is paying a minimum sum of Rs. 1,50,000/- to the legal dependants of the Advocate who expires on the next day of enrolment. The slab is the same for Death and Retirement Claim.


  1. The Schedule is as under:

  2. 1. Not exceeding ten years Rs. 1,50,000/-

  3. 2. Exceeding ten years but not exceeding twenty years Rs. 2,00,000/-

  4. 3. Exceeding twenty years but not exceeding thirty years Rs. 2,50,000/-

  5. 4. Exceeding thirty years but not exceeding forty years Rs. 3,50,000/-

  6. 5. Exceeding forty years Rs. 4,00,000/-

An Advocate can take voluntary retirement from the Fund on attaining the age of 75 years or on completion of 50 years of practice or on permanent disability which renders him totally incapable from practicing as an advocate and certified as such by the District Surgeon of the District where the Advocate is practicing.
The Advocates who have enrolled after the age of 40 years, becomes Member to the Fund after the age of 40 years, voluntarily suspended practice for employment, avocation or business, enrolled after retirement, dismissal or removal from service, becomes Member on or after 1.8.2000 and has attained an age of sixty years, becomes Member on transfer from other States after attaining the age of 40 years.

In the beginning the Membership was optional. But, unfortunately large number of legal fraternity had not taken the benefit of the scheme and therefore the Bar Council had decided to make the Membership compulsory and also affixing the stamp from the year 1997.

Annual Subcription :
An advocate who have put less than 15 years of practice shall pay a sum of Rs. 500/- and more than 15 years or practice shall pay a sum of Rs. 1000/- on or before 31st December of every year.
These amounts can be paid with Late fee of Rs. 50/- per month from January to June of the succeeding years.
Defaulting Members shall be treated as non practicing advocates for the purpose of calculation of the claim amount and a sum of Rs. 5000 shall be deducted for each default.

Those advocates who are not paying annual subscription fee continuously for 5 years treated as non practicing advocates and they will not be eligible for full benefit. However the families of such advocates will be entitled for a sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- only.

Medical Claim :
   Members of the Fund once in life time, are entitled to draw a maximum sum of Rs.1,00,000/- out of the amount payable to them under Sec. 16 of the Act,
if Member suffering from serious ailment and has completed five years of practice as a Member of the Fund on the date of treatment.

Financial Hardship :
   The Members who have attained the age of 70 years, 20 years of practice as an advocate and 12 years as Member of the Fund facing financial hardship can draw the amount of Rs. 1,00,000/- out of his fund payable under Sec. 16 of the Act. Annual Subscription fees shall be payable through online with service fee of 3%. Procedure for claiming amount :
The Claim applications will be placed before the Welfare Fund Trustee Committee constituted by the Council.

Any aggrieved person may appeal before the Karnataka State Bar Council on the decision of the Trustee Committee and the decision of the Bar Council is final.
All the funds are audited every year through Chartered Accountant of Bar Council.
Audit statements are also published in the website for a period of three months commencing from the date of approval of the audit by the Bar Council.
